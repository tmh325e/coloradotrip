Half Day (Morning or Afternoon)
🚗 Peak to Peak Scenic Byway Free Drive Kid Friendly
Colorado's first scenic byway — 55 miles from Estes Park to I-70. About 3 hours round trip. Stops include Lily Lake (flat trail, stunning Longs Peak views), St. Malo's Chapel on the Rocks (built into a cliff face), and the funky mountain town of Nederland.
🌐 Route Guide 📍 Lily Lake 📍 Nederland
Anytime
🏔️ Rocky Mountain National Park — Bear Lake $35/car entry Kid Friendly
No timed entry required in March. Bear Lake and Sprague Lake are flat, easy, paved loops with incredible mountain reflections. Good chance of seeing elk right from the road. Consider the $80 Annual NPS Pass for multiple visits.

👻 The Shining Tour — The Stanley Hotel Confirmed · $90
3 tickets confirmed. Conf: THSHNNGTR3020NFF01SL. Explore the haunted hallways that inspired Stephen King while he stayed here in 1974. The tour covers Room 217 (the real Room 237), the hedge maze, the Gold Room, and the hotel's eerie history.
🌐 Tour Details
After Tour (noon-ish)
🧊 International Cryonics Museum Kid Friendly
Right at The Stanley — this is where Grandpa Bredo (the Frozen Dead Guy!) now lives, submerged in liquid nitrogen in the hotel's old ice house. Your son will love this. The backstory ties directly into Frozen Dead Guy Days this weekend.
Afternoon
🛍️ Riverwalk & Downtown Estes Free Kid Friendly
Browse the Riverwalk shops, grab a treat, and enjoy the mountain scenery. Taffy shops, outdoor gear stores, galleries, and views of the Big Thompson River.
🌐 Riverwalk Info 📍 Directions
Evening (if clear skies)
🌌 Dark Sky Stargazing Free
Estes Park sits at 7,522 ft with minimal light pollution. On a clear March night, the Milky Way can be visible with the naked eye. Drive just outside town toward RMNP for the best views. Bring coats — it'll be cold!
